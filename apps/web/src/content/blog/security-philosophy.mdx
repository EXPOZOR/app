## Why "privacy by default" isn't enough

Most apps promise privacy in their marketing. Fewer deliver it in their architecture. The difference matters because promises can change with new management, a funding round, or an acqui-hire. Architecture is harder to change.

Our goal is to design EXPOZOR so that the data you store is protected at every layer — not just by policy, but by system design.

## Encryption in transit

All communication with EXPOZOR uses HTTPS. HSTS is enabled so your connection is always secured. We do not serve any authenticated content over plain HTTP.

## No bank credentials

EXPOZOR does not ask for, collect, or store your bank login credentials, passwords, or PINs. You add data manually, by uploading files, or by importing CSV exports you download directly from your own bank.

## No money movement

EXPOZOR cannot and does not initiate any financial transfers, payments, or transactions on your behalf. It is a read-only organizational tool.

## Analytics and tracking

We do not include third-party advertising trackers. If we use any analytics tools, they will be disclosed on our [Subprocessors](/legal/subprocessors) and [Cookie Policy](/legal/cookies) pages.

## Your data, your choice

- **Export** your data from Settings → Privacy at any time.
- **Delete** your account and all associated data by contacting support.
- Detailed data retention practices are described in our [Privacy Policy](/legal/privacy).

## Responsible disclosure

If you find a security vulnerability, email [security@expozor.com](mailto:security@expozor.com). We take all reports seriously and will respond promptly.

## What we're working toward

We are building toward a published security whitepaper and formal infrastructure audit. Details will be added to our [Security page](/security) as they are confirmed.
