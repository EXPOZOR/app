## The problem with expense trackers

I have tried the spreadsheet-heavy tools, the apps that ask for bank credentials before they show value, and the systems that make every coffee feel like admin work.

The pattern is always the same: you start motivated, you enter expenses for a week, and then you stop. Not because you do not care about your money, but because the tool demands more energy than the insight it returns.

## What if the app adapted to you?

That question started EXPOZOR. What if expense tracking could stay manual and controlled, but still feel organized?

Here is the product direction:

- **Manual entry first.** Add only the expenses you choose to track.
- **Rules before AI.** If you set a rule, that rule should take priority over a suggestion.
- **Shared notes, not payments.** Household tracking should help people remember shared expenses without initiating payments between users.
- **Upload and import when ready.** Receipt upload and CSV import are planned, but they should not require a bank connection.

## Built for real life

Money is personal. The product should feel calm and competent, not like a financial advisor or a banking product.

That means:

1. **Low-friction capture** - manual entry first, with receipt capture planned.
2. **Honest AI** - AI-assisted categorization is planned as a suggestion workflow, not a black box.
3. **Privacy-conscious defaults** - the current public site stores waitlist data only, uses HTTPS, and does not sell your data.

## What is next

EXPOZOR is waitlist-only today. The waitlist is open, no credit card is required, and paid billing is not active.
