## The problem with expense trackers

We've tried them all. The spreadsheet warriors. The apps that make you categorize every coffee. The ones that sync your bank but then show you a wall of uncategorized transactions you'll never clean up.

The pattern is always the same: you start motivated, you enter expenses for a week, and then you stop. Not because you don't care about your money — but because the tool demands more energy than the insight it returns.

## What if the app adapted to you?

That's the question that started EXPOZOR. What if instead of _you_ learning the app's system, the app learned _yours_?

Here's what that looks like in practice:

- **Your rules run first.** If you've told EXPOZOR that anything from "Tesco" is groceries, it never second-guesses you. AI only fills in the gaps.
- **Confidence is transparent.** When the AI categorizes a transaction, it shows you _why_ — and if it's less than 60% confident, it asks rather than assumes.
- **Shared expenses just work.** Split a utility bill with your household? EXPOZOR tracks who owes what without a separate app, a group chat, or a spreadsheet.

## Built for real life

We designed EXPOZOR around one principle: **money is personal.** The app should feel like a calm, competent assistant — not a financial advisor who won't stop talking.

That means:

1. **Fast capture** — snap a receipt, and on-device OCR reads it before you've put your phone away.
2. **Honest AI** — categorization that shows its work, not a black box.
3. **Privacy by default** — your financial data is encrypted with per-user keys. We can't read it even if we wanted to.
